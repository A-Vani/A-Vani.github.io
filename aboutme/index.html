Akash (Devnagri: आकाश) means “space” or “sky” and my full name, Akash Vani, means “a message from heaven”. If you are from the Indian subcontinent, you might be already familiar with my name being similar to the radio broadcasting station and/or the Hindi movie. If you are not, now you know!

I am a PhD researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics (MPA), Garching, Germany working in the Galaxy Formation and Evolution group. Currently, I am trying to understand the redshift evolution (0<z<10) of various galaxy scaling relations derived from different observations in a semi-analytical galaxy evolution model called the Munich model or L-Galaxies. I completed my master’s degree with a specialisation in Stellar, Galactic and Extragalactic astrophysics from the University of Heidelberg, Germany in September 2022. My research was focused on the properties of stars and mainly white dwarfs around the sun in a semi-analytical Milky Way model called the JJ model. This work was supervised by Apl. Prof. Dr. Andreas Just and Dr. Kseniia Sysoliatina.

I was born in the town of Mapusa in the paradise state of Goa-India. I completed my schooling at New Goa’s G.S. Amonkar Vidya Mandir (until class 10th) and later at St. Xavier’s Higher Secondary School (until class 12th). I am also a self-trained watchmaker in quartz-based and automatic wristwatches. I then moved to the city of Pune (also known to be the educational capital of India) to pursue my undergraduate degree. Where I obtained my bachelor’s degree with a major in physics and minor in mathematics and electronic sciences from Savitribai Phule Pune University in June 2019.
