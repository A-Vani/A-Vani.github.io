Properties of stellar populations and white dwarfs in a semi-analytic Milky Way disk model based on Gaia DR3

Last updated on Mar 26, 2023
CMD produced by JJ model

This work presents the extension to the white dwarfs (WD) of the semi-analytic chemo-dynamic model of the Milky Way disc, first presented by Just and Jahrei√ü (2010), called the JJ model. First, we test the JJ model‚Äôs calibration and find the inconsistency in the total star counts to be about 0.2 % and 2.2 % in 25 pc and 60 pc volume based on Gaia EDR3, respectively, implying an excellent data-to model consistency. We then enable the model to synthesise WDs using BaSTI DA and DB (carbon-oxygen core) WD isochrones and an initial-final mass relation. We also model the stellar lifetimes and produce an analytical fit to model the DA/DB ratio as a function of Teff. Finally, we also derive a volume complete Gaia DR3 WD sample in 60 pc and find that the JJ model over-predicts WDs by 52 %. This inconsistency can be linked to the assumed properties of the initial mass function (IMF) and star formation rate (SFR). Thus, the text briefly explores the model‚Äôs dependence on the IMF. A test IMF is derived; however, a thorough MCMC-based model calibration is required. This extended JJ model constitutes an excellent tool for deriving MW evolution relations such as the IMF and SFR and studying the kinematics of WDs.
